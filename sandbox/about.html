About Me

Background

I am a professor in the English department at Texas Christian University and a creative practitioner with over 30 years of experience exploring the intersections between traditional literary forms and emerging digital media.

My work spans three art forms: poetry published in prestigious journals like The Sun Magazine and Florida Review, experimental digital literature projects featured in New River: Digital Art and Literature, and musical collaborations including current work with The Deep Sleepers.

This portfolio is part of my work teaching WRIT40363, where I am the instructor in the process of learning how to teach web application development to my students, applying semantic HTML and professional web development practices.

Skills & Learning

My creative and technical skills span HTML & CSS coding, digital storytelling, multimedia poetry creation, and integrating technology into humanities education. I work with Python, JavaScript, TinkerCAD, SketchFab, and multi-track recording technologies.

As a published poet and digital humanities scholar with works in The Sun Magazine, Florida Review, and New River, I bring both traditional literary expertise and cutting-edge digital experimentation to my teaching. Students consistently praise my clear grading criteria and accessible teaching style.

My creative practice includes collaborative projects like "The Renewable Sonnets of William Shakespeare" (interactive poetry using Python + JavaScript) and "What Falls Away Is Always" (3D text sculptures). I also maintain an active musical collaboration with The Deep Sleepers, available on Spotify and Apple Music.

Goals & Interests

My goal is to continue exploring what the kids call being a "creative" (as opposed to "creator") through innovative intersections of traditional poetry, digital literature, and experimental sound. I'm particularly interested in how emerging technologies can enhance rather than replace human creativity and critical thinking.

Current projects include collaborative musical work with The Deep Sleepers (2016-2022), ongoing digital literature experiments, and developing new pedagogical approaches that bridge traditional humanities with digital making.

My creative portfolio demonstrates 30+ years of practice across 3 art forms with infinite possibilities, from prose poems published in The Sun Magazine to interactive 3D text sculptures rendering Theodore Roethke's poetry as navigable digital space.
